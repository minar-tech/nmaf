(10) Enjoining what is right in the terminology of jurists: is an order to follow Muhammad, may God bless him and grant him peace, and his religion that he brought from God. The origin of what is right is everything that is known and done beautifully and not ugly by the people of faith, and they do not find its action objectionable (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 6, p. (247).

- Enjoining good: is encouraging everything that should be said or done according to the rules of Islam (“Islamic Jurisprudence and its Evidence” by Al-Zuhayli, Vol. 8, p. 6369, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Syria - Damascus).
