(56) Ibn Abbas said: God commanded the believing women, when they went out of their homes for a need, to cover their faces from above their heads with the jilbabs and to show one eye. Muhammad bin Sirin said: I asked Ubaidah al-Salmani about the statement of God Almighty: “and to draw their cloaks over their heads,” so he covered his face and head and showed his left eye (Mukhtasar Tafsir Ibn Kathir, Vol. 2, p. (114). Publisher: Dar al-Qur’an al-Karim, Beirut - Lebanon.

- On the authority of Aisha, she said: “The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, used to pray the dawn prayer, and believing women would attend with him, wrapped in their cloaks, then they would return to their homes and no one would recognize them” (Sahih al-Bukhari, Hadith No. (372).

- On the authority of Umm Salamah, she said: “When this verse was revealed: ‘And let them draw their outer garments over their bosoms,’ some women from the Ansar came out wearing black cloaks on their heads.” Abu Bakr said that this verse indicates that a young woman is commanded to cover her face from strangers and to display modesty and chastity when going out so that people with suspicion will not covet them (Ahkam al-Qur’an by al-Jassas, vol. 3, p. (486). Publisher: Dar al-Kutub al-Ilmiyyah, Beirut, Lebanon.

On the authority of Abu Hurairah, on the authority of the Prophet, may God bless him and grant him peace, who said: “It is not permissible for a woman who believes in God and the Last Day to travel a day’s journey except with a mahram” (Sahih Muslim, (1339).

On the authority of Abu Hurairah, who said: The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said: “A woman should not travel a day and a night’s journey unless she has a mahram with her.” Agreed upon (Mishkat al-Masabih, p. (221).

Al-Ayni said: “It is permissible for a woman to go out for what she needs of permissible matters, on condition that she is presentable, has coarse clothing, does not exude flatulence, and has covered her limbs, is not flaunting her adornments or raising her voice.” (Umdat al-Qari, vol. 19, p. 125, quoting the Kuwaiti Encyclopedia of Jurisprudence, vol. 19, p. (109) Amir Hamza Library.

- (His statement: “On a journey”) is three days and nights, so it is permissible for her to go out for less than that for a need without a mahram. It was narrated from Abu Hanifa and Abu Yusuf that it is disliked for her to go out alone for a day’s journey, and the fatwa should be based on this because of the corruption of the time. Sharh al-Lubab, and it is supported by the hadith in Sahih al-Bukhari and Muslim: “It is not permissible for a woman who believes in Allah and the Last Day to travel for a day’s journey and a night’s journey except with a mahram.” In a wording by Muslim, “a night’s journey,” and in another wording, “a day” (al-Durr al-Mukhtar and Ibn Abidin’s Commentary, Vol. 2, p. (465). Publisher: Dar al-Fikr, Beirut.

Al-Aini said: “It is permissible for a woman to go out for what she needs of her permissible affairs, on condition that she is presentable, has rough clothing, does not exude flatulence, has covered her limbs, is not flaunting her adornments, and does not raise her voice” (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 19, p. (109) Amir Hamza Library.
