(138) And whoever is occupied with something, its knowledge and rulings are imposed upon him in order to refrain from what is forbidden in it. End quote. (Radd al-Muhtar, Vol. 1, p. 42; Publisher: Dar al-Fikr - Beirut).

The muhtasib should be knowledgeable about what he commands and prohibits. (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 17, p. 244; Al-Rashidiya Library).

The knowledge that must be achieved by the muhtasib is of two types:

The first type: He must be knowledgeable about the rulings of the Sharia so that he knows what it commands and prohibits, because someone who is ignorant of it may approve of what the Sharia has deemed ugly and commit a forbidden act without being familiar with the knowledge of it.

The second type: that he knows the nature of the change, such that he knows or is convinced that his denouncing of evil will remove it and that his commanding of good will have an effect on it and be beneficial. (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 17, p. 236; Al-Rashidiya Library).
