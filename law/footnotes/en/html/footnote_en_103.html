((103) “O you who have believed, indeed, intoxicants, gambling, [sacrificing on] stone alters [to other than God], and divining arrows are but defilement from the work of Satan, so avoid it that you may be successful.” (Surat Al-Ma’idah, verse (90)

On the authority of Abu Hurairah, who said: “The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, forbade pouring fermented wine into the naqir, the mazfat, the daba’, and the hantamah.” He said: “Every intoxicant is forbidden.” (Sunan Ibn Majah, Hadith No.: 3401, Publisher: Dar Ihya’ al-Kutub al-Arabiyyah - Faisal Issa al-Babi al-Halabi).

(Article (34): What is forbidden to take is forbidden to give.

(Article (35): What is forbidden to do is forbidden to seek. (Majallat al-Ahkam al-Adliyyah, Publisher: Noor Muhammad Karkhana Tejarat Books, Aram Bagh, Karachi).

Ibn Abbas said: “Wine is forbidden in and of itself, whether in small amounts or in large amounts, and intoxication from any drink” (Al-Sunan Al-Kubra by Al-Bayhaqi, Vol. 10, p. 361, Publisher: Dar Al-Kutub Al-Ilmiyyah, Beirut - Lebanon).

Among those who explicitly declared the prohibition of hashish, opium, and marijuana is Al-Haddadi in Al-Jawhara fi Akhir Al-Ashribah (Al-Bahr Al-Ra’iq, a commentary on Kanz Al-Daqa’iq - Dar Al-Kutub Al-Ilmiyyah, Vol. 3, p. 432, Beirut - Lebanon).

In Islamic jurisprudence: “Drugs and intoxicants are of many types. People use them under different names, and some of them resort to taking things that achieve the intended goal of clouding the mind. They all share one ruling, which is prohibition due to the harm they cause, which is certain to occur.”

The most famous types of drugs are: hashish, opium, cocaine, morphine, henbane (a poisonous plant used in medicine as an anesthesia), nutmeg (the fruit of a tree), barash (a compound of opium and henbane), and qat (a plant whose leaves are chewed, a small amount of it is a stimulant and a stimulant, and a large amount of it is a narcotic and a depressant, causing laziness and lethargy, and disrupting work). And other things that are taken by injection, chewing, smoking, or otherwise, lead to the loss of reason, harming health, and corrupting morals. The legal ruling on drugs is that they are forbidden except in the case of treatment due to necessity or need” (Islamic Jurisprudence, Vol. 7, p. 5512, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr, Syria - Damascus).
