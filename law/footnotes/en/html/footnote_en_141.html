(141) The right of the Imam to establish regulations derived from Sharia:

The establishment of the principle of the sovereignty of Sharia does not mean depriving the Imam and those below him who rule and are in authority of the right to make decisions and regulations that are necessary for the conduct of the affairs of the state. This is because the texts of Sharia are limited and finite, while the incidents, developments in life, and issues that face the nation and the state together are unlimited and infinite. The Imam and those in authority must confront all of that with the regulations they see fit. (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 25, p. (301).
