(7) The Muhtasib: He is the one appointed by the Imam or his deputy to look into the conditions of the subjects, investigate their affairs and interests, examine the conditions of the market in their transactions, consider their scales and their fraud, take into account what applies to their affairs, call on the violators to repent, warn them of punishment, and punish them according to what is appropriate in punishment for the crime (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 17, p. (234).

- The Muhtasib comes from Ihtisab , which means seeking reward. In Islamic law, he is the one who commands what is right if it is apparent that it has been abandoned and forbids what is wrong if it is apparent that it has been done (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 16, p. (268).

One of them: Its imposition is obligatory on the muhtasib by virtue of guardianship, and its imposition on others is included in the communal obligations (“Al-Ahkam Al-Sultaniyyah” by Al-Mawardi, p. 349, publisher: Dar Al-Hadith - Cairo).
