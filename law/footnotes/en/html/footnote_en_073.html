(73) On the authority of Saeed bin Al-Musayyab (Al-Musayyab) that he heard Abu Hurayrah, may God be pleased with him, say: The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said: “A person should not buy over the sale of his brother, and do not engage in najsh, and a city dweller should not sell to a nomad” (Narrated by Al-Bukhari: (2160).

- “And he disliked najash” with two fat-has and then with a sukoon, meaning that he increases the price without intending to buy or praises it for something that is not in it in order to promote it. (Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar, Vol. 5, p. (101). Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr for Printing and Publishing, Beirut.

In Islamic law: Increasing the price of a commodity by someone who does not want to buy it so that someone else will buy it. It is called this because the naajsh arouses desire for the commodity. He said in Al-Nihaya: It is to praise the commodity so that it will be spent and promoted or to increase its price while he does not want to buy it so that someone else will buy it. It is used in marriage and other matters. The difference between it and bargaining is that the naajsh does not desire the thing while the bargainer desires it. (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 37, p. (159).
