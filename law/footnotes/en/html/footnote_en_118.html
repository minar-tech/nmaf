((118) “And those who harm believing men and believing women for [something] other than what they have earned have certainly borne upon themselves a slander and a manifest sin.” (Al-Ahzab, verse (58)

- On the authority of Abu Hurairah, who said: The Messenger of Allah, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, said: “Do not envy one another, do not outbid one another, do not hate one another, do not turn your backs on one another, and do not undercut one another. Rather, be, O servants of Allah, brothers. A Muslim is a brother to a Muslim. He does not wrong him, nor does he abandon him, nor does he look down on him. Piety is here,” and he pointed to his chest three times. “It is enough evil for a man to look down on his Muslim brother. Everything of a Muslim is sacred to another Muslim: his blood, his wealth, and his honor.” (Narrated by Muslim, 6706; at-Tirmidhi, 1927; and Ahmad ibn Hanbal, (7713)

On the authority of Abu Burdah, on the authority of Abu Musa, he said: I said: O Messenger of God, which Islam is best? He said: “The one from whose tongue and hand the Muslims are safe” (Sahih al-Bukhari).

On the authority of Ubadah bin al-Samit, that the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said: “There should be neither harm nor reciprocating harm.”

On the authority of Abu Hurairah, that the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said: “Everything of a Muslim is sacred to another Muslim: his blood, his wealth, and his honor” (Sunan Ibn Majah).
