(37) Yes, if two just men inform him from the beginning, without seeking information, that someone is drinking alcohol in his house or that there is alcohol in his house that he has prepared for drinking, then he may enter his house and he does not have to ask permission, and the trespassing on his property by entering to prevent the evil, such as breaking his head by beating him, is for prevention, no matter how much he needs it. If two just men or one just man inform him, and in general everyone whose narration is accepted but not his testimony, then there is a question and possibility regarding the permissibility of attacking his house based on their statement, and it is better for him to refrain because he has the right not to be trespassed on his house without his permission, and the right of a Muslim is not dropped from what his right is proven against him except with two witnesses, so this is the most appropriate thing to be rejected in it. It has been said that the inscription on the ring of Luqman is: “Concealing what you have seen is better than broadcasting what you think” (“Ihya’ ‘Ulum al-Din” by al-Ghazali, vol. 2, p. 329, publisher: Dar al-Ma’rifah - Beirut).
