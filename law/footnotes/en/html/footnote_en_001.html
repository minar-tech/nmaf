(1) “In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful”: The book begins with the Basmalah first, then it is followed by the Hamdulillah, following the example of the Noble Book that begins thus, and in accordance with the saying of the Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him: “Every important matter that is not begun with the remembrance of Allah and the Name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful, is cut off.” Narrated by Al-Hafiz Abdul-Qadir Al-Rahawi in his Forty Hadiths. In the narration of Abu Dawud and Al-Nasa’i: “Every speech that is not begun with the praise of Allah is cut off.” In the narration of Ibn Majah: “Every important matter that is not begun with the praise of Allah is cut off.” Narrated by Abu ‘Awanah and Ibn Hibban in their Sahihs (Al-Bina’ah Sharh Al-Hidayah, Vol. 1, p. (105), Publisher: Dar Al-Kutub Al-‘Ilmiyyah - Beirut, Lebanon.
