(50) (His statement: “As for our time, it is forbidden for young women”) not because it is shameful, but for fear of temptation. (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar and Ibn Abidin’s Commentary”, Radd Al-Muhtar, Vol. 6, p. 370, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Beirut)

(He was forbidden to look at her face) The permissibility of looking is conditional upon the absence of desire, otherwise it is forbidden. This was in their time. As for our time, it is forbidden for a young woman. (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar”, Vol. 9, p. 532, Al-Rashidiya Library)
