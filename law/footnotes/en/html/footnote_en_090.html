((90) “Those who, if We establish them in the land, establish prayer and give zakah and enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong. And to Allah belongs the outcome of all matters.” (Al-Hajj, (41)

“And let there be among you a community inviting to what is good, enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong, and those will be the successful.” (Surat Al Imran, verse (104)

Abu Saeed said: “As for this one, he has fulfilled what was required of him. I heard the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, say: ‘Whoever among you sees an evil, let him change it with his hand. If he is unable to do so, then with his tongue. If he is unable to do so, then with his heart, and that is the weakest of faith.’” (Sahih Muslim, Hadith No. 49, Vol. 1, p. 69, Publisher: Dar Ihya’ al-Turath al-Arabi - Beirut).

The one who is to be held accountable is the one who enjoins good and forbids evil (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia”, Vol. 17, p. 261, Rashidiya Library - Kuwait and “The Greatest Treasure in Enjoining Good and Forbidding Evil”, Vol. 1, p. (57).
