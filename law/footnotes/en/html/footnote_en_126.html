(126) The Lawgiver intended to burn down the homes of those who fail to attend congregational prayer, and this is a principle in the punishment in money if he sees that (Umdat al-Qari Sharh Sahih al-Bukhari, Vol. 9, p. 243, Multan - Pakistan).

- On the authority of Omar, may God be pleased with him: He burned the house of the wine-maker. And on the authority of Imam al-Zahid al-Saffar: He ordered the destruction of the house of the immoral person because of his immorality (thus in al-Khulasah, al-Fatawa al-Hindiyah, vol. 5, p. 353, publisher: Dar al-Fikr).

- And like Omar pouring milk mixed with water for sale, and a group of jurists issued a fatwa on this, and likewise destroying adulterated goods in industries such as poorly woven clothes (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 17, p. 266, Al-Rashidiya Library).

- Punishment may be by beating, imprisonment, destruction, killing, or exile (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 17, pp. 255-(256).
