(28) The fifth condition: Ability. Ibn al-Arabi said: As for ability, it is a principle and is present in the soul, and it is present in the body if he needs to forbid it with his hand. If he fears that he will be beaten or killed by changing it, then if he hopes that it will go away, then it is permissible according to most scholars to rush in due to this uncertainty. If he does not hope for it, then what is the benefit in it? Then he said: If the intention is pure, then let him rush in however it is and not care. And he believes that saving a human being is more obligatory than saving the right of God Almighty (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 17, p. (239).
