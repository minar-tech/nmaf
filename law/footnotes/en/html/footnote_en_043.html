(43) Enjoining what is right should not entail the loss of something more beneficial or the occurrence of something more reprehensible, and forbidding what is wrong should not entail the occurrence of something more reprehensible or the loss of something more beneficial (“Hisbah in Islam” by Al-Maraghi, p. (12).

- Second: That denouncing evil leads to a greater evil, such as forbidding the drinking of alcohol, and his forbidding of that leads to killing a person, then this is forbidden in his case. And seeking reward is reprehensible if it leads to falling into something reprehensible. (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia” Vol. 17, p. (230) Al-Rashidiya Library.
