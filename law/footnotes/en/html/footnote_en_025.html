(25) The third condition: knowledge. The knowledge that must be achieved by the muhtasib is of two types: The first type: that he be knowledgeable of the rulings of the Sharia so that he knows what it commands and prohibits, because the one who is ignorant of it may approve of what the Sharia has deemed ugly and commit the forbidden act even though he is not familiar with the knowledge of it (“The Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 17, p. (235).

- “Whoever among you sees” meaning: knows “an evil” meaning: in other believers. The address is to the Companions in principle and to others from the nation as a consequence. The use of the partial “whoever” indicates that it is a communal obligation, and hints that it is only undertaken by someone who knows the levels of excellence and the differences in evil, and distinguishes between that which is agreed upon and that which is disputed among them (“Marqat al-Mafatih Sharh Mishkat al-Masabih”, vol. 8, p. 3208, publisher: Dar al-Fikr, Beirut - Lebanon).
