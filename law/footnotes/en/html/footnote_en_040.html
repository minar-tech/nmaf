- If the benefits and harms come together, and it is possible to achieve the benefits and prevent the harms, then this should be done in compliance with the command of Allah the Almighty, as He says: {So fear Allah as much as you are able} ((4). If prevention is not possible, then the harm should be prevented even if the benefit is lost. Allah the Almighty says: {They ask you about wine and gambling. Say, "In them is great sin and [yet, some] benefit for people. But their sin is greater than their benefit} (5:(17). Wine and gambling are forbidden because their harm is greater than their benefit. If pure harms come together, if it is possible to prevent them, then they should be prevented. If it is not possible to prevent them all, then the more corrupt should be prevented, then the more corrupt, and the more vile, then the more vile. If they are equal, then one may stop, or one may choose, and equality and disparity may differ. ("The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia", vol. 17, p. 231, Al-Rashidiya Library).
