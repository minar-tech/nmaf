(26) The second type: that he knows the nature of the change, such that he knows or is convinced that his denouncing of the evil will remove it and that his commanding of what is right will have an effect on it and be beneficial (“The Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 17, p. (236).

- “Whoever among you sees” i.e. knows “an evil” i.e. in other believers. The address is to the Companions in principle and to others from the nation as a consequence. The use of the partial “whoever” indicates that it is a communal obligation, and hints that it is only undertaken by someone who knows the levels of excellence and the differences in evil, and distinguishes between that which is agreed upon and that which is disputed among them (“Marqat al-Mafatih Sharh Mishkat al-Masabih”, vol. 8, p. 3208, publisher: Dar al-Fikr, Beirut - Lebanon).
