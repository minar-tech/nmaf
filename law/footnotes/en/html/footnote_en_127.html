(127) On the authority of Al-Fudayl ibn Iyad: “For the love of God and as a prisoner” (on the authority of Al-Hasan): The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, would be brought a prisoner and he would hand him over to some of the Muslims and he would say: “Be kind to him.” So he would stay with him for two or three days, and he would prefer him over himself (Tafsir Al-Zamakhshari, Al-Kashshaf ‘an Haqa’iq Ghawamidh At-Tanzil, Vol. 4, p. 668, Publisher: Dar Al-Kitab Al-Arabi – Beirut).

- The sixth type: seeking help and referring the matter to the ruler and the imam due to his general consideration and the influence of the word, unless necessity calls for abandoning support with him due to fear of missing the change, then the muhtasib must do what the need calls for at the moment. The jurists have said that the muhtasib may take whatever he requires of the matters of hisbah in what he sees as the good of the subjects and the deterrence of the corrupt, and for this purpose - in particular - he has the right to impose a punishment for every sin for which there is no prescribed punishment or expiation, which does not fall within the jurisdiction of the judge. The punishment may be by beating, imprisonment, destruction, killing, or exile (Kuwaiti Jurisprudence Encyclopedia, Vol. 17, pp. 255-(256).
