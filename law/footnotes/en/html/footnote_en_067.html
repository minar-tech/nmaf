- O you who have believed, spend from the good things which you have earned and from that which We have produced for you from the earth... (Al-Baqarah / (297).

- On the authority of Ibn Abbas, may God be pleased with them both, that the Prophet, may God bless him and grant him peace, sent Muadh, may God be pleased with him, to Yemen and said: “Call them to testify that there is no god but God and that I am the Messenger of God. If they obey that, then inform them that God has enjoined upon them five prayers every day and night. If they obey that, then inform them that God has enjoined upon them charity from their wealth, to be taken from their rich and given to their poor.” (Narrated by al-Bukhari: 1395, Muslim: 130, al-Tirmidhi: 625, and Ibn Majah: (1783)

He said: “Zakat is obligatory for a free person,” meaning it is an obligation imposed by the Book, which is the Almighty’s saying: {And give zakat}, and the well-known Sunnah, which is “Islam is built on five,” the hadith and the consensus of the nation, no one has denied it since the time of the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, until this day. (Al-Inayah Sharh Al-Hidayah, Vol. 2, p. (154). Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr.

- Zakat: It is obligatory for a free, adult, sane Muslim if he owns the minimum amount of the property in full and a year has passed. (Hanafi jurisprudence, p. 110, Book of Zakat and Al-Lubab in Explanation of the Book, p. (68). Publisher: Dar Al-Kitab Al-Arabi.
