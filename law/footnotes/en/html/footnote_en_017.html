(17) Details of the workers’ duties according to what was stated in the orders of Omar (may God be pleased with him): Everyone who was appointed as a worker would receive a written order that included his appointment, powers, and duties, and the testimony of many of the immigrants and supporters is proven in this matter (“Al-Farouq” by Shibli Al-Nu’mani, p. 224, Dar Al-Salam for Publishing and Distribution).

Since the king cannot provide all of these interests himself, he must have assistants to meet every need. One of the conditions for assistants is trustworthiness and the ability to carry out what they are commanded to do (“The Convincing Proof of God,” Vol. 1, p. 98, Publisher: Dar Ihya’ al-Ulum - Beirut, Lebanon).
