(49) In Al-Muntaqa, a young woman is forbidden from revealing her face lest it lead to temptation. In our time, this is obligatory, indeed an obligation, due to the prevalence of corruption. On the authority of Aisha, may God be pleased with her, the entire body of a free woman is private, except for one of her eyes only, in order to avoid necessity.
(“Majma’ al-Anhar fi Sharh Multaqa al-Abhur”, Vol. 1, p. 81, Chapter on the Conditions of Prayer, Publisher: Dar Ihya’ al-Turath al-Arabi)

- He was forbidden from looking at her face, so looking is permissible only if there is no desire, otherwise it is forbidden. This was in their time. As for our time, it is forbidden for a young woman. (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar”, Vol. 9, p. 532, Al-Rashidiya Library)

- The private parts of a free woman that can be seen outside of prayer are her entire body in front of foreign men. (“Islamic Jurisprudence and Its Evidences”, Vol. 1, p. 750, Publisher: Dar Al Fikr, Damascus, Syria)

The doctrine followed by later scholars is that it is disliked for women to attend congregational prayers, even for Friday prayers, Eid prayers, or sermons, absolutely, even if they are old women at night, due to the corruption of the times and the spread of immorality. (“Islamic Jurisprudence and Its Evidences,” Vol. 2, p. 1172, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr, Damascus, Syria)
