(29) What is meant by etiquette: Adopting what is praiseworthy in word and deed, and adorning oneself with noble morals. The muhtasib should adhere to it so that his work is acceptable and his words are heard (“The Kuwaiti Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 17, p. (244).

- The conditions for the Hisbah governor are: that he be free and just, have an opinion, be frank and harsh in religion, and be knowledgeable about apparent evils. These controls provide him with prestige, help him suppress evil, and make his word influential and his words heard. There is no room for debate regarding what he orders or forbids, because the important thing is to achieve the purpose of his existence, demonstrate the sanctity of the Sharia, spread virtue, and respect public morals and ethics (“Islamic Jurisprudence and its Evidences”, Vol. 8, p. 6263, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Syria - Damascus).
