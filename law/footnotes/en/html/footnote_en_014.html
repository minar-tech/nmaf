(14) Ta’zir: It is a punishment for a sin for which there is usually no prescribed punishment or expiation, whether it is a right of God Almighty or of a human being. Whoever looks at the punishment says: It is a punishment without a prescribed punishment, or he says: A punishment not determined as a right of God Almighty or of the servant (“The Jurisprudential Encyclopedia,” Vol. 25, p. (296).

- (Ta’zir) (in Islamic law) is a discipline that does not reach the legal limit, such as the discipline of someone who insults without slander (“Al-Mu’jam Al-Wasit”, Vol. 2, p. (598). Ta’zir (is) linguistically discipline (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar and Ibn Abidin’s Commentary (Radd Al-Muhtar)”, Vol. 4, pp. 40-59, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Beirut).

- Unlike the discretionary punishment that is due to the slave for slander and the like, since it depends on the lawsuit, it is only carried out by the judge unless they rule on it (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar and Ibn Abidin’s Commentary (Radd Al-Muhtar)”, Vol. 4, p. 65, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Beirut).

- The apparent meaning of their words here is that politics is an action by the ruler for the benefit he sees, even if there is no partial evidence for that action (“Al-Bahr Al-Ra’iq, a commentary on Kanz Al-Daqa’iq, Manhat Al-Khaliq, and Takmilat Al-Tawri”, Vol. 5, p. 11, Publisher: Dar Al-Kitab Al-Islami).

- (His saying “and he establishes it, etc.”) meaning the discretionary punishment that is due as a right to God Almighty, because it is a matter of removing evil, and the Lawgiver has entrusted everyone with that, as he - may God bless him and grant him peace - said: “Whoever among you sees an evil, let him change it with his hand, and if he is unable to, then with his tongue,” the hadith, unlike the prescribed punishments, the authority to which has not been proven except for the governors, and unlike the discretionary punishment that is due as a right to the slave for slander and the like, because it depends on the lawsuit, it is only established by the judge unless they rule on it (“Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar and Ibn Abidin’s Commentary (Radd Al-Muhtar),” vol. 4, p. 65, publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Beirut).
