(142) Likewise, if they command them to do something, and they do not know whether it will benefit them or not, then they must obey them, because the obligation of obedience is established by a definitive text, and the opinion that they have repeatedly held about whether what they commanded will benefit them or not is not valid in opposition to the definitive text. (Explanation of Al-Seer Al-Kabeer, Vol. 1, p. 165, Publisher: Eastern Advertising Company).

He said in “Al-Miraj”: “Because obeying the Imam in what is not a sin is obligatory.” (Radd Al-Muhtar ala Al-Durr Al-Mukhtar, Vol. 2, p. 172, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr for Printing and Publishing - Beirut).

- The requirement that obedience to the Imam is obligatory. His saying: “The order of the Sultan is to be carried out,” meaning it is to be followed and it is not permissible to disobey it. (Ibn Abidin’s commentary, Al-Fikr, Vol. 5, p. 422, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr for Printing and Publishing).

The Imams agreed that the perfect Imam must be obeyed in everything he orders unless it is a sin, and that the rulings of the Imam, his deputy, and those who follow him are binding. (Fiqh according to the four schools of thought, Book of Punishments, Vol. 4, p. 1364, Al-Haqqaniyya Library - Janak District).

- Then the laws and duties issued by the ruler become obligatory to implement. (Islamic Jurisprudence and its Evidence, Vol. 8, p. 312, Publisher: Dar Al Fikr - Syria - Damascus).
