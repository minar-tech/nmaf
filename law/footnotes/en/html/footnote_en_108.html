(108) On the authority of Abu Hurayrah: “The Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, said: ‘By the One in Whose Hand is my soul, I was thinking of ordering firewood to be gathered, then ordering the call to prayer to be made, then ordering a man to lead the people in prayer, then going to some men and burning their houses down. By the One in Whose Hand is my soul, if one of them knew that he would find a fat vein or two good spears, he would attend the ‘Isha’ prayer.” (Narrated by al-Bukhari, 644; and Ahmad ibn Hanbal, (15419)

- (His saying: And he is blamed) meaning he is reproached with the letter ta’ and not punished, as he stated in al-Bahr wa al-Nahr. However, in al-Talwih: “Abandoning the confirmed Sunnah is close to being forbidden and deserves to be deprived of intercession,” based on his saying - may the peace and blessings of God be upon him: “Whoever abandons my Sunnah will not attain my intercession.” End quote. And in al-Tahrir: “The one who abandons it deserves to be misguided and blamed.” End quote. What is meant is abandoning it without an excuse by way of insistence (Radd al-Muhtar ala al-Durr al-Mukhtar, vol. 1, p. 104, publisher: Dar al-Fikr, Beirut).
