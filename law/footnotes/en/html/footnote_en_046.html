(46) Hisbah: It is enjoining what is right if it is openly abandoned, and forbidding what is wrong if it is openly done. God Almighty said: {And let there arise out of you a community inviting to good, enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong} (Al Imran: (104).

Although this is true for every Muslim, the difference between the volunteer and the one who does it for the sake of God is in nine aspects:

One of them: Its imposition is obligatory on the muhtasib by virtue of guardianship, and its imposition on others is included in the communal obligations.

- The second: The person performing the duty of the muhtasib is one of his rights of action, from which he may not be distracted, and the person performing the duty of the volunteer is one of his voluntary acts, from which he may be distracted by something else.

Source: “Al-Ahkam Al-Sultaniyya” by Al-Mawardi (p. (349) Publisher: Dar Al-Hadith - Cairo.
