(74) Ibn Al-Musayyab said that Abu Hurairah, may God be pleased with him, said: I heard the Messenger of God, may God bless him and grant him peace, say: “An oath is a cause of increase in goods and a cause of loss of blessings” (Narrated by Al-Bukhari: (2087).

On the authority of Abdullah bin Abi Awfa, may God be pleased with him, that a man displayed a commodity while he was in the market and swore by God that he had given something for it that he had not given in order to make a Muslim man fall for it, so the following verse was revealed: {Indeed, those who exchange the covenant of God and their oaths for a small price} (Narrated by Al-Bukhari: (2088).

- In another narration: “Beware of swearing too much in sales, for it will lead to failure and then it will be lost.” (Al-Munfiqah and Al-Mumhiqah, with the first and third letters pronounced with a fat-ha and the second letter pronounced with a sukun). This includes the prohibition of swearing too much in sales, for swearing without need is disliked. This is joined to promoting the product, and the buyer may be deceived by the oath. (Al-Nawawi’s Commentary on Sahih Muslim, Vol. 11, p. (44). Publisher: Dar Ihya’ Al-Turath Al-Arabi - Beirut.
