(18) “Those who, if We establish them in the land, establish prayer and give zakah and enjoin what is right and forbid what is wrong. And to Allah belongs the outcome of all matters.” (Surat Al-Hajj, verse (41)

Hisbah: It is enjoining what is right if it is openly abandoned, and forbidding what is wrong if it is openly done. God Almighty said: “And let there be among you a group inviting to what is good, enjoining what is right and forbidding what is wrong, and those will be the successful.” (Al Imran: (104)

- And even if this is true for every Muslim, the difference between the volunteer and the one who does it for the sake of God is in nine aspects:

One of them: Its imposition is obligatory on the muhtasib by virtue of guardianship, and its imposition on others is included in the communal obligations.

- Second: The muhtasib’s performance of it is one of his rights of action, from which he may not be distracted, and the volunteer’s performance of it is one of his voluntary acts, from which he may be distracted by something else (“Al-Ahkam Al-Sultaniyyah” by Al-Mawardi, p. 284, publisher: Dar Al-Kutub Al-Ilmiyyah - Beirut, Lebanon).
