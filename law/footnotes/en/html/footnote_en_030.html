(30) Sincerity: to intend the work for the sake of God alone (“Safwat al-Tafaseer”, Vol. 1, p. 87, Publisher: Dar al-Sabuni for Printing, Publishing and Distribution - Cairo).

- It must have two things: that it be intended for the sake of God, and that it be in accordance with the Sharia in word and deed, good words and righteous deeds (“Islamic Jurisprudence and Its Evidences,” Vol. 8, p. 6261, Publisher: Dar Al-Fikr - Syria - Damascus).

Ibn Abd al-Salam said: Sincerity is for the person obligated to do an act of obedience purely for the sake of God Almighty alone, not seeking thereby to be glorified or respected by people, nor to bring about religious benefit or ward off worldly harm. It has ranks, including doing it out of fear of God’s punishment, and doing it out of glorification of God, awe, submission and response, and no matter what happens to him, rather he worships his Lord as if he sees Him, and when he sees Him, all the universes disappear from him and all the things cease to exist (“The Jurisprudential Encyclopedia”, vol. 42, p. (89).
